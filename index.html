Showup — Child Safety Standards

Standards against Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) · Last updated:

Showup is a dating application intended exclusively for adults aged 18 and over. We have zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE), child sexual abuse material (CSAM), and any content or conduct that endangers children. This page sets out the standards we apply to keep minors off our platform and to detect, remove, and report CSAE.

1. Prohibited content and conduct

The following are strictly prohibited on Showup and will result in immediate removal and account termination:

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) in any form, including real, computer-generated, animated, or AI-generated imagery depicting minors.
  • Sexualization of minors, including sexual commentary about, or sexualized depictions of, anyone under 18.
  • Grooming, solicitation, or any attempt to engage, lure, or coerce a minor into sexual activity or the production of sexual content.
  • Sextortion, trafficking, or facilitation of the sexual exploitation of minors.
  • Use of the service by anyone under the age of 18, or misrepresentation of age to access the service.

2. Minimum age and age assurance

  • Showup is restricted to users 18 years of age or older. Users must confirm they meet this requirement at registration.
  • We act on any signal that a user may be a minor, including profile information, user reports, and content review, by removing the account.
  • Accounts reasonably believed to belong to a minor are suspended pending review and terminated where confirmed.

3. Prevention, detection, and moderation

  • We maintain in-app mechanisms for users to report profiles, messages, images, and behavior that may involve CSAE.
  • Reported content is reviewed promptly, and content that violates these standards is removed.
  • We may use automated and manual review to detect prohibited content and conduct.
  • We take enforcement action including content removal, account suspension, and permanent banning of offending users and associated accounts.

4. Reporting to authorities

When we become aware of apparent CSAM or the sexual exploitation of a child, we preserve the relevant data as permitted by law and report it to the appropriate authorities. In the United States this includes reporting to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline. We cooperate with law enforcement and respond to valid legal requests.

5. How users and the public can report CSAE

Anyone can report suspected CSAE on Showup, in addition to the in-app reporting tools, by contacting our Trust & Safety team:

Email: trust-safety@showup.app

If a child is in immediate danger, contact your local emergency services first. In the U.S. you may also report directly to the NCMEC CyberTipline at report.cybertip.org or call 1-800-843-5678.

6. Designated point of contact

Showup maintains a designated point of contact for child-safety matters, reachable at trust-safety@showup.app, for users, the public, and authorities to raise concerns regarding CSAE on the platform.

7. Compliance with applicable laws

Showup complies with all applicable child-safety laws in the jurisdictions where it operates, and with Google Play's Child Safety Standards policy. These standards are reviewed and updated periodically.
